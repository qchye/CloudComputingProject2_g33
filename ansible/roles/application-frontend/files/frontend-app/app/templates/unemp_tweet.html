Relationship between Unemployment and Gig Economy related tweets

presented by Forever_33

{% if session.logged_in == NULL %}


The trend here is not obvious, but if we look deeper, we can observe that while the unemployment rises steeply from 2011 to 2015, the rate starts decelerating in 2014. This coincides with the albeit slight acceleration in the number of tweets until 2015 and 2016 where the rise continues to accelerate. This rise in tweets could be linked to, initially, a deceleration in unemployment and finally a reduction. The obvious explanation to this is that the gig economy is funding those added jobs. It is also known that the gig economy is on the rise in recent years, and the rise in the tweets could represent the rise in demand for the services that these gig economy services provide. This in turn means that there is an increased opportunity for employment in the businesses that offer these services. As a result, we see the unemployment numbers begin to curb as the popularity of the gig economy rises.
