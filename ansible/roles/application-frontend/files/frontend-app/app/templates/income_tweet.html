Gig Economy Related tweets vs Mean Income

presented by Forever_33

How the mean income in states relates to the popularity and hence, demand for gig economy services

{% if session.logged_in == NULL %}


A comparison of the mean income of a state against the number of tweets from that state relating to the gig economy. Upon initial viewing, it is obvious that Victorians have the highest volume of tweets relating to the gig economy in addition to possessing the second highest average income. NSW on the other hand has a relatively low average income but produces the second highest number of tweets. Both these states far exceed their counterparts in the number of tweets. This is most likely due to the higher population in these states, which leads to an increase in demand for businesses that rely on the gig economy. If the effect of population density is ignored, we can observe that there seems to be a general trend that the states with the higher incomes generally tweet more about the gig economy. Victoria and NSW has a comparable population and we notice that Victoria tweets more. WA and Queensland too have similar populations and yet again the pattern repeats, we see a greater numberof tweets in Queensland when it has a higher mean income. The reason this pattern exists is perhaps because it is likely that higher income people will be more willing to pay someone else to do a task, hence contributing to the gig economy.
